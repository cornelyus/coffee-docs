Water makes up ~98% of your cup. Its mineral composition affects extraction efficiency, flavor, and equipment longevity. The SCA recommends specific ranges for each mineral.

## SCA Water Quality Guidelines

| Parameter | SCA Target | Acceptable Range |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) | 150 mg/L | 75–250 mg/L |
| Calcium Hardness | 68 mg/L | 17–85 mg/L |
| Total Alkalinity | 40 mg/L | at target |
| pH | 7.0 | 6.5–7.5 |
| Sodium | 10 mg/L | &lt;30 mg/L |
| Chlorine | 0 mg/L | 0 mg/L |

<Callout type="warning">
  Tap water quality varies enormously by city. Always check your local water report or use a TDS meter before assuming your water is suitable.
</Callout>

## The Key Minerals

### Calcium & Magnesium (Hardness)

These minerals are the primary extractors — they bind to flavor compounds in coffee and pull them into solution.

- **More Ca/Mg** → stronger extraction, more body, can increase bitterness
- **Too soft** → flat, underextracted, lacking complexity
- **Too hard** → harsh, scale buildup in machines

Magnesium is more selective — it preferentially extracts sweeter, more desirable flavors. Some specialty coffee water recipes optimize for magnesium over calcium.

### Bicarbonate (Alkalinity)

Bicarbonate acts as a buffer — it neutralizes acid in your cup.

- **High alkalinity** → mutes acidity, flat cup, can taste chalky
- **Low alkalinity** → bright, acidic cup, flavor is more transparent
- **Zero alkalinity** → can make acidic coffees uncomfortably sharp

Target ~40–70 mg/L for most filter coffee.

### Sodium

Small amounts of sodium round out flavor and can enhance sweetness. Above 30 mg/L it starts to taste salty.

## Practical Water Options

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| Filtered tap (Brita, etc.) | Convenient | Inconsistent, may not remove enough |
| Third Wave Water | Purpose-made, consistent | Costs money, requires distilled base |
| Custom mineral mix | Maximum control | Time-consuming, needs distilled water |
| Bottled still water | Easy, consistent | Environmental impact |

## Soft vs. Hard Water Adjustments

If your water is **too hard** (>200 mg/L TDS):
- Use a 50/50 blend of tap and distilled
- Install a water softener or RO filter + remineralize

If your water is **too soft** (&lt;75 mg/L TDS):
- Add Third Wave Water mineral packets to distilled
- Or use: 1.5g Epsom salt + 1g baking soda per 1L distilled water (approximation)

<Callout type="tip">
  A cheap TDS meter (€10–20) is one of the highest-ROI purchases in home coffee. Measure your tap water first — you might already be in range.
</Callout>
