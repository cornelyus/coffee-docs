Espresso is coffee brewed by forcing hot water under pressure (typically 9 bar) through finely ground coffee. It's the foundation of most café drinks and one of the most technically demanding brew methods.

<BrewRecipe ratio="1:2" temp="90–94°C" grind="fine" time="25–30 sec" yield="36g (from 18g dose)" />

## The Espresso Ratio

The most important variable in espresso is the **brew ratio**: the weight of your coffee in (dose) vs. coffee out (yield).

- **Ristretto** — 1:1 to 1:1.5 (thick, intense)
- **Normale** — 1:2 (balanced, most common)
- **Lungo** — 1:2.5 to 1:3 (lighter, more extraction)

A typical single shot uses 9g in / 18g out. A double uses 18g in / 36g out.

<Callout type="info">
  Always weigh your output, not just your input. Yield matters as much as dose for consistency.
</Callout>

## Dialing In

"Dialing in" is the process of adjusting grind size to hit your target extraction time and yield.

### The Dial-In Loop

```
Shot too fast (under ~22 sec) → grind finer
Shot too slow (over ~32 sec) → grind coarser
Shot on time but tastes off → adjust dose or yield ratio
```

### Shot Diagnosis

| Result | Likely Cause |
|--------|-------------|
| Sour, sharp, thin | Under-extracted — grind finer |
| Bitter, dry, harsh | Over-extracted — grind coarser |
| Salty | Possible channeling or bad water |
| Flat, no sweetness | Stale beans or too light a roast for espresso |

## Channeling

Channeling occurs when water finds a path of least resistance through the puck rather than flowing evenly. It causes uneven extraction — parts of the coffee are over-extracted while others are under-extracted.

**Prevention:**
- Use a WDT tool (Weiss Distribution Technique) to break up clumps before tamping
- Tamp level and with consistent pressure (~15–20 kg)
- Use a puck screen or distribution tool

<Callout type="warning">
  Channeling is the #1 cause of inconsistent espresso. A distribution tool is worth the investment.
</Callout>

## Milk Steaming

For milk drinks, steam at a temperature of **60–65°C**. Whole milk produces the best microfoam for latte art.

- **Flat white / latte** — silky microfoam, little texture
- **Cappuccino** — dry foam, more airy, 1:1:1 ratio of espresso:milk:foam
