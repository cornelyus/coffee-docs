Pour over brewing produces some of the clearest, most nuanced cups in coffee. Water is poured manually over a bed of grounds in a filter, and gravity pulls the brewed coffee through.

## Hario V60

The V60 is the benchmark pour over brewer. Its large central hole and spiral ribs give you maximum control — and maximum room for error.

<BrewRecipe ratio="1:15" temp="93°C" grind="medium-fine" time="3:00–3:30" />

### Technique

1. Rinse the filter with hot water (removes paper taste, preheats the vessel)
2. Add your ground coffee, tap to level
3. **Bloom** — pour 2× the coffee weight in water, wait 30–45 seconds. This lets CO₂ escape for more even extraction.
4. Continue pouring in slow, concentric circles every 45 seconds until target weight is reached
5. Total draw-down should finish at 3:00–3:30

<Callout type="tip">
  If it's drawing down too fast, go finer. Too slow, go coarser. Aim for the last drop to fall just as you hit your target weight.
</Callout>

## Chemex

The Chemex uses a thicker paper filter that removes more oils, producing an exceptionally clean, tea-like cup. It's also gorgeous on a coffee table.

<BrewRecipe ratio="1:15" temp="94°C" grind="medium-coarse" time="4:00–4:30" />

### What Makes Chemex Different

The thick bonded filters mean slower flow and higher clarity. Use a slightly coarser grind than V60. The Chemex works particularly well with lighter roasted, high-acidity coffees like Ethiopian naturals.

## Kalita Wave

The Kalita Wave has a flat-bed design with three small holes. This creates a more forgiving, even extraction compared to the V60's single central hole.

<BrewRecipe ratio="1:15" temp="93°C" grind="medium" time="3:00–3:30" />

### Why the Wave is Beginner-Friendly

The flat bed levels the extraction across the entire coffee bed rather than funneling water to a center point. Small variations in pour technique have less impact on the final cup.

## Grind Size Guide

| Brewer | Grind | Particle Size |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| Chemex | Medium-coarse | ~800–1000µm |
| Kalita Wave | Medium | ~600–800µm |
| V60 | Medium-fine | ~400–600µm |

<Callout type="warning">
  Always grind immediately before brewing. Pre-ground coffee goes stale within minutes.
</Callout>
