In immersion brewing, coffee grounds steep in water for a set amount of time before being separated. This produces a fuller body and more even extraction than percolation methods.

## French Press

The French press is the classic immersion brewer. Metal mesh filter means oils pass through, contributing to a thick, full-bodied cup.

<BrewRecipe ratio="1:15" temp="94°C" grind="coarse" time="4:00" />

### Technique

1. Preheat the press with hot water, discard
2. Add coarse ground coffee
3. Pour all water at once, stir gently
4. Place the lid on (don't press yet), steep for 4 minutes
5. Press slowly and pour immediately — don't let it sit or it will over-extract

<Callout type="tip">
  For a cleaner cup, let the press sit for 5 minutes after plunging to let sediment settle, then pour gently.
</Callout>

### Common Issues

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Gritty sediment | Grind too fine | Go coarser |
| Weak, watery | Too little coffee or short steep | Adjust dose or time |
| Bitter | Over-extraction, grind too fine | Coarser grind, pour immediately |

## AeroPress

The AeroPress is the most versatile brewer ever made. It uses air pressure to push water through coffee, producing a concentrated, clean cup in under 3 minutes.

<BrewRecipe ratio="1:12" temp="85–95°C" grind="medium-fine" time="2:00–3:00" />

### Standard Method

1. Place filter in cap, rinse it, attach to chamber
2. Add ground coffee (inverted or standard orientation)
3. Pour water to target weight, stir once
4. Steep 1–2 minutes
5. Press slowly over 30–45 seconds

<Callout type="info">
  The AeroPress World Championship has produced hundreds of radically different winning recipes. Experiment freely — there's no wrong answer.
</Callout>

### Inverted vs. Standard

- **Standard**: risk of early drip-through; faster
- **Inverted**: no drip-through, more control over steep time

## Clever Dripper

The Clever Dripper combines the clarity of paper filtration with the control of full immersion. It only flows when placed on a cup, giving you an immersion steep with a filter's clean finish.

<BrewRecipe ratio="1:15" temp="93°C" grind="medium" time="3:30–4:00" />

### Why the Clever Dripper

It's the most hands-off of the immersion brewers:

1. Add filter, rinse, add coffee
2. Pour all water, place lid, steep for 3 minutes
3. Set on cup — gravity opens the valve
4. Drain completes in ~45 seconds
