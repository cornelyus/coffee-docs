Processing refers to how the coffee cherry is removed from the bean after harvest. It's one of the biggest determinants of flavor, often more influential than origin or variety.

## The Coffee Cherry

A coffee bean is actually the seed inside a fruit — the coffee cherry. From outside to inside:

1. **Skin** (exocarp)
2. **Pulp** (mesocarp)
3. **Mucilage** — sticky, sugar-rich layer
4. **Parchment** (endocarp)
5. **Silver skin** (spermoderm)
6. **Bean** (seed)

Processing methods differ in how much of these layers are removed before drying.

## Washed (Wet) Process

The skin and pulp are removed before drying. Beans dry on raised beds with only parchment remaining.

**Flavor profile:** Clean, bright, high acidity, transparent origin character. What you taste is almost entirely the bean.

**Common origins:** Ethiopia (Yirgacheffe), Colombia, Kenya, Guatemala

<Callout type="tip">
  Washed coffees are the best for evaluating terroir — you get a clear window into the origin character.
</Callout>

## Natural (Dry) Process

The whole cherry dries intact — no pulp removal. This can take 4–6 weeks.

**Flavor profile:** Fruity, wine-like, heavy body, fermented notes. Strawberry, blueberry, and tropical fruit are common descriptors.

**Common origins:** Ethiopia (Harrar, Sidama), Brazil, Yemen

<Callout type="info">
  Natural processing is the oldest method. It predates electricity or running water — just sun and time.
</Callout>

## Honey Process

A middle ground: skin is removed but some or all mucilage is left on the bean during drying. Named for the sticky texture during drying, not sweetness.

### Honey Levels

| Type | Mucilage Remaining | Body | Acidity |
|------|--------------------|------|---------|
| Yellow Honey | ~25% | Light | High |
| Red Honey | ~50% | Medium | Medium |
| Black Honey | ~75–100% | Full | Low |

**Common origins:** Costa Rica, El Salvador, Panama

## Anaerobic (Fermentation) Process

Beans ferment in sealed, oxygen-free tanks before (or instead of) traditional processing. CO₂ builds up and creates distinctive flavor compounds.

**Flavor profile:** Intense, complex, often winey or funky. Can taste of whiskey, tropical fruit, or spice.

<Callout type="warning">
  Anaerobic coffees are polarizing. They can be extraordinary or unpleasantly fermented depending on process control and your taste preferences.
</Callout>

## Processing Comparison

| Method | Clarity | Body | Sweetness | Risk |
|--------|---------|------|-----------|------|
| Washed | High | Light | Medium | Low |
| Natural | Low | Full | High | High (ferment defects) |
| Honey | Medium | Medium-Full | High | Medium |
| Anaerobic | Varies | Varies | Very high | High |
