## Choosing a Brewing Method

Every brewing method is a tradeoff between **control**, **convenience**, and **cup character**. The two fundamental categories are:

- **Percolation (filter)** — Water passes through a bed of coffee. Clean, transparent, highlights acidity and delicate flavors.
- **Immersion** — Coffee steeps in water. Fuller body, more even extraction, more forgiving.

## Brewing Method Comparison

| Method | Category | Clarity | Body | Difficulty | Time |
|--------|----------|---------|------|------------|------|
| V60 | Percolation | High | Light | Medium | 3–4 min |
| Chemex | Percolation | Very high | Light | Medium | 4–5 min |
| Kalita Wave | Percolation | High | Light-Med | Low | 3–4 min |
| French Press | Immersion | Low | Full | Low | 4 min |
| AeroPress | Immersion/Hybrid | Medium | Medium | Low | 2–3 min |
| Espresso | Pressure | N/A | Very full | High | 25–30 sec |

## Key Variables in Any Brew

Regardless of method, four variables determine your cup:

1. **Dose** — Amount of coffee (grams)
2. **Water** — Amount (grams) and temperature (°C)
3. **Grind size** — Fine extracts more, coarse extracts less
4. **Time** — Total contact time between water and coffee

<Callout type="tip">
  Change one variable at a time when dialing in a new coffee. That way you know what caused any change in the cup.
</Callout>

## Extraction

Extraction is the percentage of soluble material dissolved from your grounds into the cup. The SCA's ideal range is **18–22%** extraction.

- **Under-extracted** (&lt;18%) → sour, sharp, thin
- **Ideal** (18–22%) → balanced, sweet, complex
- **Over-extracted** (&gt;22%) → bitter, harsh, dry
