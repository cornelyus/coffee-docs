Coffee is grown in the "Bean Belt" — a band between the Tropics of Cancer and Capricorn. Within this zone, altitude, soil, rainfall pattern, and varietal combine to create dramatically different flavor profiles.

## The Bean Belt

Major growing regions:

- **Africa** — Ethiopia, Kenya, Rwanda, Burundi, Tanzania
- **Americas** — Colombia, Brazil, Guatemala, Honduras, Peru, Panama
- **Asia/Pacific** — Indonesia, Yemen, India, Papua New Guinea

## Why Altitude Matters

Higher altitude = lower temperature = slower cherry ripening = more sugar development = more complex flavor.

| Altitude | Typical Character |
|----------|-------------------|
| &lt;1000m | Mild, low acidity, earthy |
| 1000–1500m | Balanced, medium acidity |
| 1500–2000m | Bright, complex, high acidity |
| 2000m+ | Exceptional complexity (rare) |

## Key Arabica Varieties

| Variety | Origin | Flavor Notes |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| Typica | Ethiopia (ancestral) | Clean, balanced |
| Bourbon | Réunion Island | Caramel, fruit |
| Gesha/Geisha | Ethiopia → Panama | Floral, jasmine, bergamot |
| SL28/SL34 | Kenya | Blackcurrant, tomato, fruit |
| Pacamara | El Salvador | Full body, complex |
| Caturra | Colombia | Bright, citric |

<Callout type="info">
  Gesha (also spelled Geisha) from Panama's Hacienda La Esmeralda sparked the modern specialty coffee boom after winning the 2004 Best of Panama auction at a then-record price.
</Callout>

## Flavor by Region (General Patterns)

| Region | Acidity | Body | Common Notes |
|--------|---------|------|-------------|
| Ethiopia | High | Light | Floral, berry, citrus |
| Kenya | High | Medium | Blackcurrant, tomato, bright |
| Colombia | Medium-High | Medium | Caramel, red fruit, nutty |
| Brazil | Low | Full | Chocolate, nut, caramel |
| Guatemala | Medium | Medium | Chocolate, spice, apple |
| Indonesia | Low | Very Full | Earthy, cedar, tobacco |

These are tendencies, not rules. Processing method and roast level can override origin character entirely.
