Ethiopia is where coffee was born. Wild coffee trees still grow in the forests of Kaffa. The country is the genetic homeland of Arabica coffee and home to more heirloom varieties than anywhere else on earth.

<Callout type="info">
  Ethiopian coffees are often labeled "heirloom" because the country has thousands of indigenous varieties — most still uncharacterized by science.
</Callout>

## Key Regions

### Yirgacheffe

The most celebrated Ethiopian origin. Located in the Gedeo Zone of the Southern Nations region at 1,700–2,200m.

**Flavor profile:** Intensely floral (jasmine, bergamot), bright lemon or lime acidity, delicate tea-like body, stone fruit.

- Washed Yirgacheffe is the archetype of floral/citrus filter coffee
- Natural Yirgacheffe adds wild blueberry and strawberry on top of the floral base

### Sidama / Sidamo

South of Yirgacheffe, at 1,400–2,200m. Broader, more diverse in character than Yirgacheffe.

**Flavor profile:** Fruity and bright, but earthier and more varied than Yirgacheffe. Peach, apricot, citrus.

### Harrar (Harrar)

Eastern highlands at 1,500–2,100m. Almost always natural processed.

**Flavor profile:** Bold, wine-like, heavy body, blueberry, dark fruit, sometimes chocolatey. One of the most distinctive coffees in the world.

### Kaffa (Jimma)

The historical birthplace of coffee. Lower altitude, forest coffees.

**Flavor profile:** Wilder, more rustic, herbal, spicy. Less refined but historically significant.

### Guji

An emerging subregion of Oromia, producing some of Ethiopia's most sought-after coffees.

**Flavor profile:** Tropical fruit, berry, floral — similar to Yirgacheffe but often with more body.

## Traceability in Ethiopia

Ethiopian traceability has historically been complicated by the Ethiopia Commodity Exchange (ECX), which blended coffees from different farms. The introduction of direct trade and the Yirgacheffe Coffee Farmers Cooperative Union (YCFCU) improved lot-level traceability significantly.

Look for bags listing:
- Specific woreda (district) within the region
- Washing station or cooperative name
- Processing method

## Varietals

Ethiopian heirloom varieties include:
- **JARC varieties** (74110, 74112, 74158) — developed by the Jimma Agricultural Research Center
- **Wild forest** accessions — uncatalogued wild varieties
- **Kurume / Dega** — altitude-specific groupings

Gesha, now famous from Panama, originated in the Kaffa/Gori Gesha forest of Ethiopia.
