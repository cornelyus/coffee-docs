Cupping is the standardized method for evaluating coffee quality. It's used by roasters, importers, and Q Graders worldwide to assess and compare coffees objectively.

## The SCA Cupping Protocol

### Equipment Required

- Cupping bowls (250ml / 8oz)
- Freshly ground coffee (8.25g per 150ml water)
- Water at **93°C** (199°F)
- Timer
- Two cupping spoons
- Scoring sheets

### The Process

**1. Fragrance (dry)**
Assess the aroma of the dry grounds. Note intensity and quality.

**2. Aroma (wet)**
Pour water at 93°C and assess the steam rising from the crust. Time: 0–4 minutes.

**3. Break the crust**
At 4 minutes, push the crust with a spoon in three forward strokes. Deeply inhale the released aromas.

**4. Skim**
Remove floating grounds and foam from the surface.

**5. Taste**
Begin tasting at 8–10 minutes (when the cup has cooled to ~70°C). Aspirate coffee vigorously from the spoon — the spray coats your entire palate.

**6. Score**
Continue tasting as it cools. Flavors evolve significantly as temperature drops.

<Callout type="tip">
  The best flavors often emerge at 45–55°C. Be patient — don't judge a coffee only at tasting temperature.
</Callout>

## SCA Scoring Attributes

Each of the 10 attributes is scored 6–10 (with increments of 0.25):

| Score Range | Quality Level |
|-------------|---------------|
| 6.00–6.75 | Good |
| 7.00–7.75 | Very Good |
| 8.00–8.75 | Excellent |
| 9.00–9.75 | Outstanding |

Attributes: Fragrance/Aroma, Flavor, Aftertaste, Acidity, Body, Balance, Uniformity, Clean Cup, Sweetness, Overall.

**Total possible: 100 points.** Coffees scoring 80+ are specialty.

## The SCA Flavor Wheel

The SCA Flavor Wheel (2016 revision, developed with World Coffee Research) organizes coffee descriptors into a taxonomy:

**Outer ring → Inner ring → Center**

Examples:
- Fruity → Berry → Blackberry / Raspberry / Blueberry
- Floral → Black Tea / Jasmine / Rose
- Nutty/Cocoa → Chocolate → Dark / Milk / Cocoa
- Sweet → Vanilla / Caramel / Molasses
- Roasted → Tobacco / Pipe / Acrid

<Callout type="info">
  The flavor wheel is a communication tool, not a scoring rubric. Use it to find vocabulary for what you're experiencing.
</Callout>

## Developing Your Palate

### Triangulation

Brew three cups — two identical, one different. Try to identify the odd one out. This trains your sensitivity to small differences.

### Reference Tasting

Taste the things you expect to taste in coffee:
- **Citric acid** (lemon) vs. **malic acid** (green apple) vs. **phosphoric acid** (tropical brightness)
- **Caramel** vs. **toffee** vs. **molasses**
- Fresh berries alongside your coffees

### Keep Notes

Write down descriptors every time you taste. Even if you're wrong, the habit builds pattern recognition over time.
