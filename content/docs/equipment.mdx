Good equipment matters, but not all equipment matters equally. The hierarchy: **grinder > water > scale > brewer > kettle**.

<Callout type="tip">
  Spend money on your grinder first. A mediocre brewer with an excellent grinder will outperform the inverse every time.
</Callout>

## Grinders

Grind consistency is everything. Uneven particles mean some over-extract and some under-extract simultaneously.

### Burr Types

| Type | Pros | Cons |
|------|------|------|
| Flat burr | Consistent, unimodal distribution | Expensive, retention |
| Conical burr | Low retention, forgiving | Bimodal distribution |
| Blade (propeller) | Cheap | Avoid — produces dust and chunks |

| Budget | Model | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| €30–60 | Hario Slim / Timemore C2 | Hand grinders, surprisingly good |

| €300–600 | Niche Zero / Eureka Mignon | Excellent for espresso + filter |

## Scales

Precision is essential for repeatability. Brew by weight, not volume.

| Feature | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| Resolution | 0.1g minimum |
| Capacity | 2kg+ |
| Timer | Built-in preferred |
| Response time | Fast (Acaia Lunar, Timemore Black Mirror) |

The **Timemore Black Mirror Basic+** (~€60) is the best value. The **Acaia Pearl** is the benchmark but costs 4× more.

## Kettles

For pour over, a gooseneck kettle gives you flow control. Temperature control is useful but not mandatory if you have a thermometer.

| Model | Type | Price |
|-------|------|-------|
| Hario V60 Buono | Stovetop | €30–40 |
| Fellow Stagg EKG | Electric, temp control | €160–180 |
| Brewista Artisan | Electric, temp control | €100–130 |
| Bonavita Variable | Electric, temp control | €70–90 |

## Brewers

The brewer is the least important variable — once you have a consistent grinder and scale, almost any brewer works.

| Brewer | Price | Best For |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Hario V60 (plastic) | €8 | Maximum control, retains heat |
| Kalita Wave | €30–50 | Forgiving, consistent |
| Chemex 6-cup | €45 | Beautiful, clean cup, entertaining |
| AeroPress | €35 | Travel, experimentation, speed |
| French Press | €20–40 | Low effort, full body |
| Moka Pot | €20–40 | Espresso-adjacent, stovetop |

## Water Filtration

Filtered water is often more impactful than upgrading your brewer.

- **BWT Penguin** — popular in specialty coffee, magnesium-enriching filter
- **Peak Water Jug** — adjustable mineral content
- **Third Wave Water** — mineral packets for distilled water
- **RO + remineralization** — maximum control, significant setup cost

## Maintenance

Equipment you don't clean is equipment that degrades your coffee:

- **Grinder** — brush after each use, deep clean monthly (Grindz tablets)
- **Espresso machine** — backflush daily, descale every 3 months
- **Carafes and brewers** — rinse immediately, wash weekly
- **Scale** — keep dry, calibrate occasionally
